Tej is one of the most widely used traditional drinks in Ethiopia

What is Tej?

Tej is a traditional Ethiopian and Eritrean alcoholic beverage made from fermented honey and a mixture of hops and/or leaves from the gesho plant (Rhamnus prinoides). It is often referred to as "honey wine" and is a significant part of these countries' cultures and social gatherings.

What are the Ingredients to make Tej?

  • Honey
  • Water
  • Gesho leaves and/or hops(Gasho is a plant native to the region and contributes to the unique flavor and characteristics of the beverage)

How to make Tej?

Fermentation

Tej is created through a natural fermentation process. The honey-water mixture is left to ferment, usually for several weeks or months. During fermentation, the sugars in the honey are converted into alcohol by yeast.

Here are some key features of Tej:

  1. Flavor and Aroma

    Tej has a sweet yet tangy flavor, often with floral and herbal notes from the gesho leaves. The taste can vary depending on factors such as the type of honey used and the fermentation process.

  2. Serving and Consumption

    Tej is traditionally served in a special vessel called a "berele" or a "gesho," which resembles a long-necked vase. It is poured into small cups and consumed communally. The host of a gathering may pour the tej for their guests, and the act of pouring is often accompanied by traditional gestures and songs.

  3. Social and Cultural Significance

    Tej has a long history in Ethiopian and Eritrean cultures and is associated with various social and religious occasions. It is commonly enjoyed during festive events, celebrations, weddings, and other gatherings.

  4. Variations

    Tej can come in different varieties, ranging from dry to sweet and with varying levels of alcohol content. Some versions of tej might be brewed stronger than others
